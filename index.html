Audio Documentary

Where the Ice Feels Like Home

An audio documentary following Natalie, a competitive hockey player, through the sounds, routines, and emotional intensity of life on and around the rink.

Audio Documentary Field Recording Interview Sports Storytelling
Back to Videography

Project Overview

This documentary explores how the rink becomes a second home and why the ice keeps calling players back. It follows Natalie, a university student and competitive hockey player whose life has been shaped by years of moving between teams, positions, and roles on the ice.

The project uses sound to place the listener directly beside her, moving between quiet preparation and the high-energy intensity of a game. Through interview and field recording, it looks at how hockey becomes part of identity, routine, discipline, and community.

Where the Ice Feels Like Home

Story Focus

The documentary centers on Natalie’s everyday life as a hockey player, from packing gear and preparing mentally at the rink to the fast, loud atmosphere of a late-night game. The contrast between these quieter and more intense moments shapes the structure of the piece.

My Contribution

This project is presented as an individual audio documentary by Sara Truffi. It includes the treatment, interview development, sound planning, narrative structure, and the overall documentary concept.

A journey through the sounds of hockey, exploring how the rink becomes a second home.

Sound Design Plan

The documentary was designed around layered sound: skates gliding across ice, pucks hitting boards, sticks tapping, whistles, crowd reactions, locker room ambience, gear sounds, and bench chatter. These recordings were planned to create a strong sense of place and movement.

The sound plan moves between two different worlds: the quieter rhythm of preparation and practice, and the louder pressure of game night.

Interview Direction

The interview questions focus on Natalie’s history with hockey, her routines, the difference between games and practices, the sacrifices involved, and what being on the ice makes her feel. They also explore how hockey has shaped who she is beyond the sport itself.

Narrative Structure

The treatment is structured around a progression from opening game atmosphere, to pre-game routine, to the pressure of competition, to challenges and sacrifice, and finally back to the lasting emotional pull of the rink. It closes by returning to the sound that begins everything: the bag dropping onto the bench and the skates touching the ice again.

Outcome

This project shows my interest in sound-based storytelling and documentary work that builds emotion through rhythm, atmosphere, and voice. It highlights the kind of media work I want to continue creating: stories that feel immersive, personal, and strongly tied to place and identity.
